Bachelors + Co-term in four years: my take on Stanford Coursework

December 2025

Preface:

I kind of want to preface this that this entire website and this post especially is inspired by Chip Huyen. I think it was about three years ago when I chanced upon her website. At the time, I was still deciding on whether I should pursue premed or not.

Specfically, it was this post about the AI courses she completed at Stanford, and how she would approach them if she was a freshman again. I think overall this was crazy helpful and pretty cool for me (I remember being so surprised in how she finished everything so fast). It's kinda crazy that I am now in this position. Anyways, here's my experiences and hopefully this helps someone as much as chip's helped me.

I spent four years at Stanford going through my bachelors and masters in computer science. Coming into Stanford, I had an expectation to do a lot of things here, and that was either get into medical school (rip) or co-term in four years. It's been a journey, and it is safe to say that my parents' doctor-in-the-family dreams are over.

I remembered that back when I was a frosh, I was somewhat frustrated with the little support and resources I got from understanding college and curriculums. I spent an embarassingly long time not understanding what a credit or unit is. My personal belief is that unit counts are fake and really, no one should worry about that stuff except for the purposes of satisfying graduation requirements.

To you, the reader. Your expectations of Stanford CS are probably that it's super 'presitguous' and rigorous. Which is kind of fair, given that we are such a good institution and we have some quite famous classes (CS 230, 231N, 336, ...). I honestly think it's what you make of it.

I do want to say that Stanford has been extremely accessible to me, and I think that many people can thrive here despite the challenging workload. Feel free to reach out to me and ask any questions. I love questions from people.

Frosh Year:

I came into Stanford unsure if I wanted to do computer science. I did however have the benefit of getting some credit for MATH 19, 20, 21 through AP Calculus AB + BC and CS 106A through AP Computer Science A. I also had credit for PHYSICS 41 and 43 through AP Phsyics: Mechanims and AP Physics: E&M.

Quarter 1

  • EMED 111A
  • CHEM 31M
  • PWR 1BRB
  • CME 100

Overall, a decent quarter. It's 19 units which is on the higher end for frosh fall quarter, but it was manageable. About 2/5 difficulty. I think CHEM 31M matched AP Chem pretty well. CME 100 was honestly pretty easy. I kind of wish I took MATH 51 instead, as it was a more rigorous class.

Quarter 2

  • EMED 111B
  • COLLEGE 102
  • CS 106B
  • CS 103

I decided to take CS 106B with Keith Schwarz and CS 103 with Cynthia Lee. This was a pretty tough quarter for me, as I was continuously sick and lost motivation in week 7. I ended up having COVID during finals week and took CS 103 as an incomplete due to my sickness. It was also raining really heavily during finals week, to the point that campus lost power. A lot of finals were cancelled in the following hours. I ended up taking the CS 103 final in spring quarter, which I bombed.

I think difficulty was a 4/5. I really liked 106B, and it was fun-ish getting through recursion. I did a little bit of discrete mathematics in high school and came into 103 thinking I would love it. I do like 103 coursework a lot and the psets were kind of painful-fun, but I didn't do to well in the class.

Quarter 3

  • EMED 111C
  • CHEM 33
  • COLLEGE 108
  • PATH 51
  • CME 192
  • CME 193

EMED 111C ramped up in terms of work due to shadowing hours. I didn't pay too much attention in CHEM 33 due to burnout. All the other classes were pretty easy. In between classes, I was constantly trying to figure out if I wanted to really study to be a premed. Difficulty was like 1/5.

I remember the ochem final. I was just sitting in looking at the test, hearing everyone frantically write. After a few minutes, I decided to write my name on the test and go to sleep, deciding that I would no longer be premed.

I remember finishing the quarter pretty disapointed in myself. It was effectively a 'useless' quarter for me, as I had learned very little and none of the classes I took contributed to my major besides filling up unit count.

Summer 1

  • CHEMENG 12SC

I decided to do sophomore college. I did the art + chemsitry one, which is the only SOCO class that fulfills a ways requirement (CE). I had a great time with Curt and Sara.

Sophomore Year:

Quarter 4

  • CS 107
  • CS 109
  • MATH 104
  • PWR 2IY
  • ATHLETIC 60

I think this was my least favorite quarter at Stanford. Sophomore slump was real, and my schedule was not helping. CS 109 was a pretty fun and easy class for me. First pset was super fun to do. CS 107 was okay, not terrible until the end. MATH 104 was my least favorite class ever at Stanford. Overall difficulty was a 5/5 for me.

Quarter 5

  • CS 161
  • CS 224N
  • ARCHLGY 117
  • ATHLETIC 60

I had a great time this quarter. CS 161 honestly was pretty fun and intuitive. CS 224N was a good introduction to AI for me. However, I did break my ankle mid-quarter, and so it was not fun. Difficulty was a 3/5.

Note the bolded coursework. This is coursework that I later counted towards my co-term.

Quarter 6

  • ENGR 76
  • CS 278
  • CS 181W
  • CS 152
  • ATHLETIC 60

I don't think any of these classes were independtly difficult. Honestly, they were all pretty chill in terms of coding. However, there was a lot of busy work that made this quarter a bit more difficult than I expected. Difficulty was 3/5.

I got a lot out of this quarter. CS 278 was my first real taste of HCI at Stanford, and I found it to be a very intuitive and insightful class. Bernstein is such a great lecturer and his class alone convinced me to major in the HCI track for CS.

Junior Year:

Quarter 7

  • VIETLANG 1
  • CS 147
  • CS 147L
  • CS 177
  • CS 148
  • ENGLISH 50

I went full ham on the HCI track. I think 147 is the class that I got the most out of at Stanford. A lot of the HCI design principles are so intuitive and relevant to industry experience. I had an amazing group project and we did pretty well in terms of awards at the CS 147 project expo. It was a lot of work, quite literally it was the work you'd expect for a class project every week. The amount of coordination was crazy and I think everyone in the class put their best foot forward. I was geuninely surprised by the quality of work from myself and peers. Crazy good class.

CS 147L was pretty chill with really just four assignments. CS 177 was full of busywork. CS 148 was relatively chill. Overall difficulty was 4/5.

Quarter 8

  • VIETLANG 2
  • CS 229
  • CS 205L
  • CS 111
  • CS 347
  • ENGLISH 50

This was my hardest quarter at Stanford, by hours and unit count. CS 229 was a good amount of work, as I had to pull all-nighters for every pset. CS 205L was a decent amount of work every week, but it's easier with all of the encouraged collaboration. CS 111 was ok, similar to CS 107 except no hard psets towards the end. Overall difficulty was 5/5.

CS 347 kind of surprised me. I didn't know my peers in CS were so eloquent. There was so much solid discussion in section that I was geunuinely baffled by the quality.

Quarter 9

  • VIETLANG 3
  • CS 224S
  • CS 247G
  • CS 194
  • MUSIC 31N
  • CEE 33C

This was a fun quarter. CS 224S wasn't too much work and I was kind of interested in the subject. I heard a lot of good things about CS 247G, but I ultimately didn't like the class too much. CS 194 was a blast. I had an amazing team for CS 194 and we did pretty well at the project showcase. Overall difficulty was a 2/5.

In this point of time, I finished all of my graduation and major requirements for my B.S. in Computer Science on the HCI track.

Senior Year:

Quarter 10

  • VIETLANG 21
  • CS 238
  • CS 230
  • CS 300
  • CS 7

This was my lightet quarter in terms of units and hours. CS 300 was interesting to be in. I learned a decent amount in CS 7. I covered most of CS 230 when I was preparing for MLE interviews. Overall difficulty was a 1/5.

I kept this quarter intentionally light. Part of it was that it was the fall of my senior year (more social events), and the other part was to give me time to recruit for a new-grad job.

Quarter 11

  • CHINLANG 1
  • CS 246
  • CS 295
  • CS 399
  • STATS 202
